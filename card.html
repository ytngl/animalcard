Rhinoceroses

Rhinoceroses are the largest land animal after the elephant.

  • Scientific Name: Diceros bicornis
  • Average Length: 9 ft
  • Average Lifespan: 35 years
  • Habitat: Savannas

Often black or white, the Rhinoceroses inhabit Asia and Africa. They are an endangered species with less than 30,000 roaming the lands.
